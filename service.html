Qualified Doctors

Doctors are working in various departments such as emergency medicine, surgery, pediatrics, oncology, cardiology, neurology, and many others. They are responsible for providing timely and effective medical care to patients, including performing examinations, ordering diagnostic tests, prescribing medications, and creating treatment plans.

Medical Treatment

Once admitted, the patient will be assigned to a medical team, which will consist of a primary care physician, nurses, and other healthcare professionals. The team will work together to diagnose the patient's condition and develop a treatment plan. This plan may include medications, surgeries, physical therapy, or other forms of treatment, depending on the patient's needs.Throughout the treatment process, the medical team will monitor the patient's progress and adjust the treatment plan as necessary. They will also provide education and support to help the patient manage their condition and maintain their health.

Cardiology

The Cardiology Service provides a range of services that includes electrocardiograms (ECGs),stress tests,echocardiograms,and Holter monitoring.The service also provides a wide range of treatment options, including medical therapy, lifestyle changes, and surgical interventions. These may include coronary artery bypass surgery, valve repair or replacement, pacemaker implantation, and angioplasty. The Cardiology Service works closely with other departments in the hospital, such as the Intensive Care Unit (ICU), Emergency Department, and Cardiac Rehabilitation Program, to provide comprehensive care for patients with heart and cardiovascular diseases.

Medical Lab

Some of the common medical lab facilities services provided by hospitals include:
Blood testing:This is one of the most common tests carried out in medical labs. The lab analyzes blood samples to diagnose and monitor a range of conditions such as infections, anemia, and diabetes.
Urine testing:This test is used to detect and monitor conditions such as urinary tract infections, kidney disease, and diabetes.
Imaging tests:Hospitals have advanced imaging equipment such as X-rays, CT scans, and MRI machines to help diagnose and monitor conditions such as bone fractures, tumors, and internal injuries.
Microbiology:Medical labs also carry out tests to identify various types of bacteria, viruses,and other microorganisms that cause infections.
Pathology:Pathology is the study of disease, and medical labs perform tests to analyze tissue samples for cancer, infections, and other conditions.
Genetic testing:Genetic testing helps identify changes or mutations in genes that may cause diseases.

Dental Care

Dental services typically involve the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of diseases and disorders related to the teeth, gums, and mouth.Common dental services offered include:
Dental check-ups and cleanings:Regular dental check-ups and cleanings are essential to maintain good oral health and prevent dental problems such as tooth decay, gum disease, and oral cancer.
Dental X-rays:Dental X-rays are used to detect problems that may not be visible during a regular dental exam, such as tooth decay, abscesses, and impacted teeth.
Fillings and extractions:If a tooth has been damaged by decay or injury, a filling or extraction may be necessary to prevent further damage or infection.
Root canal treatment:If the pulp inside a tooth becomes infected or inflamed, a root canal procedure may be required to remove the damaged tissue and save the tooth.
Crowns and bridges:Crowns and bridges are used to restore damaged or missing teeth, and can be made from a variety of materials including porcelain, ceramic, or metal.
Dentures and implants:Dentures and implants are used to replace missing teeth, and can be customized to fit each patient's unique needs.

Surgery


General surgery:These are surgeries that deal with a wide range of medical conditions such as appendectomy, hernia repair, gallbladder removal, and more.
Orthopedic surgery:This type of surgery involves treating injuries and conditions that affect the musculoskeletal system such as fractures, joint replacements, and sports injuries.
Neurosurgery:These are surgical procedures that deal with the brain, spinal cord, and nervous system, including brain tumors, aneurysms, and spinal cord injuries.
Cardiothoracic surgery:This involves surgeries related to the heart and lungs, including bypass surgeries, heart valve replacements, and lung cancer surgery.
Gynecologic surgery:These surgeries are specific to the female reproductive system, including hysterectomy, uterine fibroid removal, and ovarian cyst removal.
Urologic surgery:This type of surgery deals with the urinary system, including kidney stone removal, prostatectomy, and bladder cancer surgery.

Pharmacy

The pharmacy facilities are essential components of the healthcare system that help ensure the safe and effective use of medications by patients.
Dispensing medications:Hospital pharmacies are responsible for filling prescriptions and dispensing medications to patients, including preparing sterile and non-sterile compounded medications.
Monitoring medication use:Hospital pharmacists work closely with healthcare providers to ensure that medications are used safely and effectively. They monitor medication orders, identify potential drug interactions, and recommend alternative therapies when appropriate.
Managing drug inventory:Hospital pharmacies are responsible for managing the inventory of medications and ensuring that medications are available when needed.
Providing patient education:Hospital pharmacists provide patient education on the safe and effective use of medications, including instructions on how to take medications and potential side effects.
Conducting medication reconciliation:Hospital pharmacists work with healthcare providers to ensure that medications are accurately and completely reconciled across different settings of care.
